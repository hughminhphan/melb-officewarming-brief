03 Brand Guidelines

Typography

TypefaceUseWeightSource
Satoshi Primary Lyra brand font — headings, body, web, all materials Regular (400), Medium (500) Fontshare

Important: Lyra uses Satoshi as its sole typeface. Do NOT use Space Grotesk, Basement Grotesque, or Helvetica Light — those belong to a separate personal brand, not Lyra.

Use Satoshi throughout. Different weights (Regular vs Medium) provide enough contrast. Do NOT use more than 2 typefaces on the poster.

Colour Palette

#000000 — Background (pure black)
#010314 — Background alt (dark navy)
#D4D4D6 — Primary text (light gray)
#9E9EA0 — Secondary text (mid gray)
#B59BFC — Accent purple (key brand accent, use at ~42% opacity for glow)
#9E48FE → #2C42E6 — Gradient accent (purple-to-blue, CTAs + highlights)
#421E6B — Deep purple (background accent zones)
#1D1D22 — Card/surface (dark charcoal)
#FAF9F5 — Warm off-white (use sparingly)

Key principle: Dark, minimal, premium aesthetic. Lots of negative space. Subtle glassmorphism (frosted glass effects, translucent borders). The boldness comes from ONE element at a time — not everything screaming.

Logo

FileSizeUse
logo-white.png981 x 360pxWhite wordmark — use on dark backgrounds
logo-black.png981 x 360pxBlack wordmark — use on light backgrounds
logo-icon.png330 x 330pxIcon mark only

Usage: Logo should be present but not dominant. Bottom or top corner placement. Let the event messaging be the hero.

Visual Style

  • Dark, minimal, premium — clean layouts, generous whitespace
  • Glassmorphism accents — subtle frosted glass effects, translucent borders
  • Border radius: 12px on cards/containers (rounded, not pill)
  • Photography: Use real team/office photos where possible
  • NO stock photos. Use the real office and real people, or abstract/geometric design elements.

05 Mood & Direction

Tone

Celebratory but not corporate. Think "house party invite designed by a tech company" not "corporate event flyer."

  • Warm — you're genuinely invited, not just filling seats
  • Confident — we're proud of the new office
  • Minimal — let the design breathe, don't overcrowd

Design References

  • Lyra's website: lyratechnologies.com.au — match this aesthetic
  • Dark backgrounds with purple accent glows
  • Clean typography with generous spacing
  • If using photography, add slight gradient overlays to maintain the dark theme

Do

  • Match the website's dark, premium aesthetic
  • Use purple accents sparingly
  • Keep generous whitespace
  • Use real Melbourne office photos

Don't

  • Make it look like a corporate annual report
  • Use bright/neon colours outside the purple palette
  • Overcrowd with text
  • Use generic event poster templates
